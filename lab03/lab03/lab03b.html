Riyadh

Overview

Riyadh is the capital and largest city of Saudi Arabia, located in the heart of the Arabian Peninsula. Known for its modern skyline, the city is a hub of business, commerce, and government, serving as the political and administrative center of the country. Riyadh is also home to numerous cultural attractions, shopping centers, and a growing expatriate community, making it a dynamic and vibrant metropolis in the Middle East.

History

Riyadh's history dates back to the pre-Islamic era, but it gained prominence in the 18th century when it became the capital of the First Saudi State under the leadership of Sheikh Mohammed ibn Abdul Wahhab and Muhammad ibn Saud. Over the years, it has witnessed significant transformations, evolving from a desert oasis into a modern metropolis, playing a crucial role in the unification and development of Saudi Arabia.

Population

Riyadh has a population of over 7 million people, making it one of the most populous cities in the Arabian Peninsula. The city has experienced significant population growth due to urbanization, economic opportunities, and government initiatives aimed at attracting foreign workers and investors. For the most up-to-date population figures, it's advisable to consult the latest demographic data or official sources.

Attractions

A fun fact about Riyadh is that it is home to the King Fahd International Stadium, one of the largest stadiums in the world with a seating capacity of over 68,000. This stadium has hosted numerous international sporting events and concerts, making it a significant entertainment venue in the region.
